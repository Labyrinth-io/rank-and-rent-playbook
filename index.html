The big idea

You are not building a website for a client.
You are building an asset you own.

The goal is to attract people searching for a specific service in a specific area, capture their calls and enquiries, and then rent that lead flow to one real business for a monthly fee. The business pays to receive the calls, forms, and enquiries. The website, the domain, and the data stay yours.

01 — Own

Keep the asset

The domain, the website, and admin access to tracking never leave your hands. Renters come and go; the asset stays.

02 — Prove

Show real demand

Build, track, and let real search data prove people want this service here — before you ask anyone to pay.

03 — Rent

Sell access, not theory

When the leads are real, offer exclusive access to one provider for a monthly fee. You rent flow, not promises.

The core principle

Build first. Track results. Prove demand.
Then rent the lead flow.

✕Never do this
  • Promise rankings
  • Fake a business or use someone's number without permission
  • Create fake Google Business Profiles or fake reviews
  • Sell theory before there's proof
✓Always do this
  • Sell access to an asset once there is proof
  • Use honest wording and real tracking
  • Get consent before routing any leads
  • Treat it like a portfolio — double down on winners

Reading the competition

Before you build, search like a customer.

Open Google and run the real searches a customer would. You're looking for cracks, not perfection.

✓Good signs — there's room
  • Old-looking websites & poor page titles
  • Thin content, few suburb-specific pages
  • Directories ranking instead of strong businesses
  • Slow sites, confusing contact options
  • No pricing guidance, no helpful FAQs
✕Bad signs — go more specific
  • Strong businesses dominate every result
  • Big brands and high-authority sites everywhere
  • Many strong, well-optimised SEO pages
  • Heavy paid-ad competition
  • The results already look excellent

Pages that rank

One asset, one simple structure.

Don't build a general site. Build one specific asset that targets one service, one main area, and several nearby suburbs.

The 10 core pages
  • Home page
  • Main service page
  • Main location page
  • Suburb pages 1, 2 & 3
  • Cost / pricing guide
  • FAQ page
  • Contact / request-a-quote page
  • Thank-you page
What every page needs
  • Clear title & H1 with service + location
  • Short intro: who it's for, when they need it
  • The process & the service areas
  • An FAQ section answering real questions
  • Strong CTA, click-to-call & quote form
  • Internal links to related pages

Use AI to draft, then improve it so it genuinely helps. Good content answers: How much does it cost? How fast can I book? Do you come to me? What suburbs are covered? What if the vehicle fails?

The metrics that matter

Read the signals. Then act.

Search Console tells you what Google is doing with your site. Each number points to a specific next move.

Impressions

Is Google testing you?

How often your site appears in search. Rising impressions mean Google is showing your pages.

Clicks

Are searchers interested?

How many people clicked through to your site. Interest you can build leads on.

CTR

Is your title attractive?

Click-through rate. Low CTR with high impressions = fix your titles and descriptions.

Avg. position

Are you climbing?

Where you rank on average. A page at 8–20 is an opportunity to push higher.

Top queries

What to build next?

The keywords you're shown for. Each new query is a page or section to create.

Indexed pages

Is Google seeing it all?

How many pages are in the index. Missing pages? Resubmit the sitemap and request indexing.

Decision rules

When something's off, do this.

Don't guess. Match the symptom to the fix.

If
No impressions after several weeks
→ then
  • Check indexing & resubmit the sitemap
  • Improve the homepage & internal links
  • Add more specific suburb / service pages
If
Impressions but no clicks
→ then
  • Improve title tags & meta descriptions
  • Make the offer clearer
  • Use stronger service / location wording
If
Clicks but no leads
→ then
  • Improve the CTA & phone-button visibility
  • Shorten the form, add trust content
  • Add pricing & fix mobile layout
If
Leads but no renter
→ then
  • Contact 20 businesses
  • Offer a 14-day lead trial
  • Show traffic screenshots & exclusive access

The rules of the game

Honest by design. Always.

This model only works long-term when it's clean. These aren't suggestions.

✕Never
  • Use a business's number without permission
  • Pretend to be a business you are not
  • Create fake Google Business Profiles
  • Use fake reviews or fake addresses
  • Sell the same exclusive leads to multiple businesses
  • Mislead customers or misrepresent results
✓Always
  • Use honest wording and track results properly
  • Keep ownership clear
  • Get consent before routing leads
  • Tell renters exactly what they're paying for
  • Define clicks vs. calls vs. leads vs. booked jobs
  • Stay accurate in every monthly report
Final word

You don't need to be an SEO genius to start. You need to build, track, learn, improve — and monetise.

Treat it like a portfolio. Some sites will do nothing. Some will show signs of life. A few become real assets. Build the assets, track the truth, double down on the winners, and rent the lead flow.
